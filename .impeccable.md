## Design Context

### Users
The primary audience for this deployment is affluent Chinese AI users who want immediate, reliable access to global mainstream AI tools without managing overseas accounts, payment methods, phone verification, network setup, or provider-by-provider configuration. This audience should guide product decisions, but the interface should not explicitly describe users by wealth or status. The job to be done is to make global AI access feel simple, trustworthy, compliant, and ready to use.

### Brand Personality
Nyquiste should feel premium, safe, and approachable. The core brand ideas are all-in-one, compliant, and convenient. The main landing-page title should remain `Nyquiste 全球 AI 全家桶`. Copy should be direct and confidence-building, avoiding hype, technical clutter, or language that sounds exclusionary.

### Aesthetic Direction
The visual direction is premium consumer subscription rather than generic enterprise SaaS. The interface should support light and dark modes equally, with a polished, high-trust feel: refined spacing, calm hierarchy, clear status signals, and concrete product previews. Existing brand/UI foundations include Public Sans, optional Lora, OKLCH theme tokens, theme presets, Tailwind, Base UI/shadcn-style components, and icon-driven controls. Avoid anything that looks obviously AI-generated: generic glow-heavy sections, decorative blobs, stock-like visual filler, overused purple/blue gradients, repetitive card grids, and vague claims without product substance.

### Design Principles
1. Make premium feel effortless: use restrained polish, precise spacing, concise copy, and concrete product details instead of loud visual effects.
2. Communicate safety without fear: emphasize compliance, stability, secure routing, and ready status through clear UI signals and measured language.
3. Keep the experience approachable: explain benefits in plain Chinese-first terms, reduce configuration anxiety, and make primary actions obvious.
4. Show the actual service: prefer dashboard previews, provider/model states, subscription details, and network status over abstract decoration.
5. Preserve accessibility and resilience: maintain WCAG AA contrast, keyboard operability, reduced-motion support, responsive layouts, and full i18n coverage.
